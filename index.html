Buzz Aldrin Meets with Senate Subcommittee on Space, Science, and Competitiveness

On Wednesday October 19, 2016, famed astronaut Buzz Aldrin met with Sen. Ted Cruz and the other members of the Senate Subcommittee on Space, Science, and Competitiveness. Aldrin, who is known for having been the second man to set foot on the moon, discussed the value of space exploration with the committee and advocated for greater NASA funding in the coming years.  Read more >

The American League for Interplanetary Exploration and Navigation, a nonpartisan interest group that advocates for greater astronautical development and more interplanetary exploration, has called for an increased amount of space-related information to be presented in the Common Core standards.  Read more >

ALIEN Receives Endorsement from Elon Musk

On Monday September 12, 2016, the American League for Interplanetary Exploration and Navigation received one of its most notable endorsements to date. Elon Musk, founder of SpaceX and dedicated advocate for the creation of a human colony on Mars, voiced his support for ALIEN through Twitter.

ALIEN Denounces Gravity: “This is the Scientifically Bankrupt Perception of Space that We’re Fighting”

The American League for Interplanetary Exploration and Navigation recently denounced Alfonso Cuaron’s 2013 film Gravity for its “scientifically bankrupt perception of space.” Spokesperson Carina Qin called for Cuaron to apologize for the scientific inaccuracies and encouraged the public to research space rather than relying on popular media narratives.  Read more >

The mission of the American League for Interplanetary Exploration and Navigation is to develop the astronautical capabilities of the United States and her allies to expand mankind to other planets, by:
  • Lobbying for greater governmental priority for interplanetary exploration,
  • Advocating for increased funding for NASA,
  • Encouraging private development of astronautical technologies, and
  • Increasing public interest in the exploration of space.
We believe that developing astronautical capabilities is essential because interplanetary habitation is the only viable path for the long-term survival of the human race, and because in pursuing interplanetary habitation, advances in science and medicine will be made that will offer potentially groundbreaking advances in human welfare.
